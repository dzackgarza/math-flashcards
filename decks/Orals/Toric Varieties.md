- What is the **character lattice** $ M $? What is its **dual lattice** $ N $?

    $ M = $ **characters**, $ N = $**cocharacters/1PSs**.

    Set $ T^n \coloneqq{\mathbf{G}}_m^n $, then $ M \coloneqq\mathop{\mathrm{Hom}}(T^n , {\mathbf{G}}_m) $ is the character lattice. Contains elements $ \chi_m $ where $ \mathbf{t} \mapsto \prod t_i^{m_i} $. Then $ N \coloneqq\mathop{\mathrm{Hom}}({\mathbf{G}}_m, T^n) $ is the lattice of cocharacters/1-parameter subgroups, contains elements $ \lambda^u $ where $ t\mapsto \mathbf{t}^u \coloneqq(t^{u_i}) $. Use the perfect pairing $ {\left\langle {m},~{n} \right\rangle} \coloneqq\ell $ where $ \ell $ is the unique integer such that $ \chi_m \circ \lambda^n: {\mathbf{G}}_m\to {\mathbf{G}}_m $ is $ t\mapsto t^\ell $. Note $ N\otimes_{\mathbf{Z}}{\mathbf{G}}_m \cong T $.

- What is a **strongly convex rational polyhedral cone**?

    **Cone**: $ {\mathbf{R}}_{\geq 0} \sigma \subseteq \sigma $.

    **Strongly convex**: $ \sigma \cap(-\sigma) = 0 $, or contains no nonzero subspace of $ N_{\mathbf{R}} $, or $ \dim \sigma {}^{ \vee }= \dim M $.

    **Rational**: generated by lattice elements $ \left\{{\mathbf{v}_i}\right\} \subseteq N $.

    **Polyhedral**: finitely generated.

- What is the **dual of a cone**?

    $ \sigma {}^{ \vee }\coloneqq\left\{{m\in M {~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}{\left\langle {n},~{m} \right\rangle} \geq 0 \,\, \forall n\in \sigma}\right\} $.

- Discuss how to construct an affine toric variety from a fan.

    $ \sigma \leadsto S_\sigma \coloneqq\sigma {}^{ \vee }\cap M \leadsto X_\sigma \coloneqq\operatorname{Spec}{\mathbf{C}}[S_\sigma] $ where $ {\mathbf{C}}[S_\sigma] \coloneqq\left\{{ \sum_{s\in S_{\sigma}} c_s \chi^s {~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}c_s\in {\mathbf{C}}\text{ and }c_s = 0 { \text{a.e.} }}\right\} $ with character multiplication.

- What is a **fan**?

    A collection of SCRPCs closed under taking faces and the intersection of any two cones is a face, where $ \tau \leq \sigma $ is a face iff $ \tau = H_m \cap\sigma $ for some hyperplane corresponding to $ m\in \sigma {}^{ \vee }\subseteq M_{\mathbf{R}} $ and $ H_m \coloneqq\left\{{n\in N_{\mathbf{R}}{~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}{\left\langle {m},~{u} \right\rangle} = 0}\right\} \subseteq N_{\mathbf{R}} $.

- What is a **convex set**?

    For all $ p,q\in S $, the line $ tp + (1-t)q\in S $. In dimension 2, equivalently for any $ \left\{{p_i}\right\} \subset S $ and any scalars $ \left\{{\lambda_i}\right\} $ with $ \sum \lambda_i = 1 $, $ p \coloneqq\sum \lambda_i p_i\in S $.

    Note $ F\leq P $ is proper iff $ F = P \cap(H_n + r)) $ for some $ r $, i.e. equal to intersection with some affine hyperplane so $ F = \left\{{p\in P {~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}{\left\langle {n},~{p} \right\rangle} = r}\right\} $.)

    Alternatively: take the normal fan of $ P $ in $ N $ to define $ X_P $

    Alternatively: $ X_P \coloneqq\bigcup_{m\in M\cap P} \operatorname{Spec}{\mathbf{C}}[\sigma_{\widehat{m}} \cap M] $ where $ \sigma_{\widehat{m}}\coloneqq{ \mathrm{Cone} }(P\cap M-m) $.

    How these are related: normal fan for $ P \cong $ proper faces over $ P^\circ $.

- Discuss the **orbit-cone correspondence**.

    Each cone $ \sigma $ determines a distinguished point $ x_\sigma \in U_\sigma $ corresponding to the map $ S_\sigma \coloneqq\sigma {}^{ \vee }\cap M \to \left\{{0, 1}\right\} $ where $ m\mapsto 1 $ if $ m\in \sigma^\perp $ and 0 otherwise where $ \sigma^\perp \coloneqq\left\{{m\in M_{\mathbf{R}}{~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}{\left\langle {n},~{m} \right\rangle} = 0\,\, \forall n\in \sigma}\right\} $. Then there is a bijection from $ \Sigma $ to $ T{\hbox{-}} $orbits in $ X_\Sigma $ where $ \sigma \mapsto T\cdot x_\sigma $ and an $ r{\hbox{-}} $dimensional cone corresponds to an $ n-r{\hbox{-}} $dimensional orbit, e.g. $ \dim \sigma =0 \rightleftharpoons{\mathbf{G}}_m^n $ and $ \dim \sigma = n \rightleftharpoons T{\hbox{-}} $invariant points, and the face poset corresponds to the orbit closure poset (contravariantly).

- What is a toric morphism?

    On lattices: images of cones are contained in cones. A map $ \phi: N_1\to N_1 $ where for each $ \sigma_1 \subseteq N_{1, {\mathbf{R}}} $ there is a $ \sigma_2 \subseteq N_{2, {\mathbf{R}}} $ with $ \phi(\sigma_1) \subseteq \sigma_2 $. At the level of varieties: $ \phi: X_{\Sigma_1} \to X_{\Sigma_2} $ a morphism of varieties with $ \phi(T_{1})\subseteq T_2 $ which restricts to a group morphism. At the level of abstract toric varieties: for $ V_i = \operatorname{Spec}{\mathbf{C}}[S_i] $ and $ \phi: V_1\to V_2 $, toric iff $ \phi^*: {\mathbf{C}}[S_2] \to {\mathbf{C}}[S_1] $ is induced by a semigroup morphism $ (\phi^*)_*: S_1\to S_2 $.

- When is a toric variety smooth?

    Iff each affine patch is smooth, and $ U_\sigma $ is smooth iff admits generators which can be extended to a basis of $ N $. In this case $ U_\sigma \cong {\mathbf{A}}^k \times {\mathbf{G}}_m^{n-k} $ where $ k = \dim \sigma $.

- When is a toric variety proper/complete?

    When $ {\left\lvert {\Sigma} \right\rvert} = N_{\mathbf{R}} $, i.e. full support. Why: each $ n\in N_{\mathbf{R}} $ determines a one-parameter subgroup $ \lambda^n $, and if $ n=(n_1, n_2) $ then one looks at $ \lim_{t\to 0}\lambda^n(t) $ -- if $ n $ is in a cone, the limit exists, else not. - When is $ D $ **globally generated/bpf**?

- When is $ D $ **ample/bpf**?

    $ {\mathcal{O}}(D) $ is ample iff $ u_{\sigma}\neq u_{\sigma'} $ for distinct $ \sigma\neq \sigma'\in \Sigma(n) $ and $ P_D $ is $ n{\hbox{-}} $dimensional with vertices $ u_\sigma $.\

- When is $ D $ **very ample**?

    $ D $ is very ample iff $ \psi_D $ is strictly convex **and** $ S_\sigma $ is generated by $ \left\{{u - u(\sigma) {~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}u\in P_D\cap M}\right\} $ where $ u(\sigma) $ is the linear function associated to $ \sigma $ defined by $ \psi_D({-}) = {\left\langle {u(\sigma)},~{{-}} \right\rangle} $ so $ D = \sum - \psi_D(\rho_i) D_{\rho_i} $ since $ \psi_D(\rho_i) = a_i $ in $ D = \sum a_i D_{\rho_i} $.

    $ \psi_D $ is **strictly convex** if convex and $ u(\sigma), u(\sigma') $ are not equal for distinct maximal cones $ \sigma,\sigma' $.

    If $ X $ is smooth complete, ample iff very ample.

- Classify smooth **Fano** toric surfaces.

    Fano polytopes: $ 0\in \mathrm{int} P $ and the vertices of every facet form a lattice basis. $ P $ is reflexive, so $ P {}^{ \vee } $ is a lattice polytope and $ X_{P {}^{ \vee }} $ is smooth toric Fano whose normal fan comes from taking cones over proper faces of $ P $ -- every smooth toric surface arises this way. - What are the **Weil divisors** of a toric variety?

    $ \operatorname{Div}_T(X_\Sigma) \cong {\mathbf{Z}}^{\Sigma(1)} $, i.e. each $ \rho\in \Sigma(1) $ corresponds to $ D_\rho $. A general Weil divisor is $ D = \sum_{\rho \in \Sigma(1)} a_\rho D_\rho $.

- What are the **Cartier divisors** on a toric variety?

    $ \operatorname{CDiv}_T(U_\sigma) \cong M/M(\sigma) $ where $ M(\sigma) \coloneqq\sigma {}^{ \vee }\cap M $, and $ \operatorname{CDiv}_T(X_\Sigma) = \cocolim_{\sigma \in \Sigma} M/M(\sigma) $. More concretely described by Cartier data $ \left\{{(U_\sigma, m_\sigma)}\right\} $ where $ { \left.{{D}} \right|_{{U_\sigma}} } = \operatorname{div}(\chi^{m_\sigma}) $ for $ m_\sigma\in M $ with $ {\left\langle {\rho },~{m_\sigma} \right\rangle} = -a_\rho $ for every $ \rho\in \Sigma(1) $, where $ D = \sum a_\rho D_\rho $.

    Can also obtain ffrom polytopes: write $ P = \left\{{m\in M_{\mathbf{R}}{~\mathrel{\Big\vert}~}{\left\langle {\mu_F},~{m} \right\rangle} \geq -a_F \,\,\text{ for all facets } F\leq P}\right\} $ where $ \mu_F $ is the minimal generator along the normal to the facet $ F $. Then take $ D \coloneqq\sum a_F D_F $ where $ D_F $ is the divisor for the ray associated to $ F $ in $ X_P $.
