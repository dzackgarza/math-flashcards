- What is a regular ring? 2023-01-1

    A local ring $ (R, {\mathfrak{m}}_R) $ is **regular** iff $ {\mathfrak{m}}_R $ can be generated by $ \dim R $ elements.

    The minimal number of generators of $ {\mathfrak{m}}_x $ equals its Krull dimension.

    An arbitrary ring $ R $ is **regular** iff $ R_p $ is regular for every prime.

- What is $ {J ({R}) } $? How is it interpreted geometrically?

    $${J ({R}) } \coloneqq\bigcap_{{\mathfrak{m}}\in \operatorname{mSpec}R} {\mathfrak{m}}= \bigcap_{M\in  {}_{R}{\mathsf{Mod}}\text{ simple}} \operatorname{Ann}_R(M)$$

    If $ R\in   {}_{k}{\mathsf{Mod}}^{{\mathrm{fg}}} $, so $ R\cong k[x_1, \cdots, x_{n}]/I $, the $ {\sqrt{0_{R}} } = {J ({R}) } $, so $ {J ({R}) } $ measures how far away $ I $ is from defining the ring of functions on some variety.

- What is a quasiregular element in a ring?

    $ a\in R $ with $ 1-a\in R^{\times} $; $ {J ({R}) } $ is the largest ideal with every element quasiregular.

- What is the Artin-Rees lemma?

    $ I{~\trianglelefteq~}R, N\leq M\in   {}_{R}{\mathsf{Mod}}\implies \exists C\in {\mathbf{Z}} $ such that

    $$I^nM = I^{n-C}(I^CM \cap N)$$

- What is Krull's intersection theorem?

    For $ I{~\trianglelefteq~}R $ Noetherian with $ I \subseteq {J ({R}) } $,

    $$\bigcap_{n\geq 0} I^n = 0$$

    Works for $ R\in \mathsf{Loc}\mathsf{CRing} $ with $ I\coloneqq{\mathfrak{m}}_R $.

- What is the geometric interpretation of Krull's intersection theorem?

    Define $ { \mathrm{Ord} }_{p} f $ as the smallest $ n $ such that $ f\in {\mathfrak{m}}_p^n $ but $ f\not\in {\mathfrak{p}}^{n+1} $; then $ { \mathrm{Ord} }_p(f) = \infty \iff f=0 $.

    There is no hypersurface passing though $ p $ with arbitrarily high order of vanishing.

    One can detect whether $ n $ functions vanishing at $ 0\in {\mathbf{A}}^n $ generate a local coordinate system (so generate $ {\mathfrak{m}}_0 $) by knowing their differentials (residues in $ {\mathbf{T}}_0 {\mathbf{A}}^n = {\mathfrak{m}}_0/{\mathfrak{m}}_0^2 $ are a vector space basis), like an implicit function theorem.

- What is a reduced ring?

    No nonzero nilpotents.

- What is Nakayama's lemma?

    $ M = IM \implies m= im $, i.e. if $ I{~\trianglelefteq~}R $ and $ M\in  {}_{R}{\mathsf{Mod}}^{\mathrm{fg}} $ with $ M=IM $, then $ \exists i\in I $ such that $ m=im $ for all $ m\in M $. So if $ I $ stabilizes $ M $, then an individual $ i\in I $ stabilizes all of $ M $.

    For $ R $ local, $ M\to N $ is surjective iff $ M/{\mathfrak{m}}_RM \to N/{\mathfrak{m}}_R N $ is surjective as a map of vector spaces.

- Give a categorical/homological corollary of Nakayama's lemma.

    For $ R $ Noetherian, $ M\in   {}_{R}{\mathsf{Mod}} $ flat, then $ M $ is free:

    Take a presentation $ K\hookrightarrow R^n \twoheadrightarrow M $, reduce to $ k $ to get $ K'\hookrightarrow k^n \twoheadrightarrow M' $ where $ K'=0 \implies K=0 $.

    Thus flat coherent sheaves over a Noetherian scheme are vector bundles.

- What is Nakayama's lemma for a local ring?

    For $ (R, {\mathfrak{m}}_R) $ a loca ring, minimal generators of $ M\in   {}_{R}{\mathsf{Mod}} $ biject with $ k\operatorname{mod} $ bases for $ M/{\mathfrak{m}}_R M $ where $ k \coloneqq R/{\mathfrak{m}}_R $.

    Any two such sets of minimal generators for $ M $ of size $ n $ are related by a matrix in $ \operatorname{GL}_n(R) $.

- What is the geometric interpretation of Nakayama's lemma?

    For any $ {\mathcal{F}}\in    {}_{{\mathcal{O}}_X}{\mathsf{Mod}} $, regard stalks $ {\mathcal{F}}_p \in   {}_{{\mathcal{O}}_{X, p}}{\mathsf{Mod}} $, then the fibers $ {\mathcal{F}}(p) \coloneqq{\mathcal{F}}_p/{\mathfrak{m}}_p {\mathcal{F}}_p $ are vector spaces and any basis for the fiber $ {\mathcal{F}}(p) $ lifts to a minimal generating set for the stalk $ {\mathcal{F}}_p $.

    If $ {\mathcal{F}}\leadsto E $ representes a vector bundle, then a basis for $ { \left.{{E}} \right|_{{p}} } $ can be lifted to a basis of global sections in some neighborhood of $ p $.

- Give a geometric application of Nakayama's lemma.

    If $ f:X\to Y $ is a projective morphism between quasiprojective varieties, then $ f $ is an isomorphism iff $ df_p $ is injective for all $ p\in X $.

- What is the going up theorem?

    If $ R\hookrightarrow S\in  {}_{{\mathbf{Z}}}  \mathsf{Alg} $ is an integral extension, then $ \operatorname{Spec}S\to \operatorname{Spec}R $, and for every $ p\in \operatorname{Spec}R $ there is a $ \tilde p\in \operatorname{Spec}S $ such that $ \tilde p \cap R = p $.

- What is Krull's theorem?

    Every nonzero ring has a maximal ideal.

    Stronger version: every proper ideal is contained in a maximal ideal (can apply to $ I=\left\langle{0}\right\rangle $).

- What is Krull's Hauptidealsatz?

    Let $ R $ be a Noetherian ring and $ a $ an element of $ R $ which is neither a zero divisor nor a unit. Then every minimal prime ideal $ P $ containing $ a $ has height 1.

- What is the one-step submodule test?

    If $ r\in R,\, m,n \in M \implies rm + n \in M $ then $ M $ is a submodule.
